Artificial Intelligence (AI) - Comprehensive Overview

1.  Introduction Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation
    of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think,
    reason, learn, and make decisions. AI systems aim to mimic cognitive
    functions such as learning, problem-solving, perception, and
    language understanding.

2.  Types of Artificial Intelligence 2.1 Narrow AI (Weak AI) Designed to
    perform specific tasks (e.g., recommendation systems, voice
    assistants, fraud detection). Examples: Chatbots, image recognition
    systems.

2.2 General AI (Strong AI) A theoretical form of AI capable of
performing any intellectual task that a human can do.

2.3 Superintelligent AI A hypothetical AI that surpasses human
intelligence across all domains.

3.  Core AI Domains 3.1 Machine Learning (ML) A subset of AI that
    enables systems to learn from data.

-   Supervised Learning
-   Unsupervised Learning
-   Semi-supervised Learning
-   Reinforcement Learning

3.2 Deep Learning Uses artificial neural networks with multiple layers.
Common architectures: - Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) - Recurrent
Neural Networks (RNNs) - Transformers - Generative Adversarial Networks
(GANs)

3.3 Natural Language Processing (NLP) Enables machines to understand and
generate human language. Applications: - Chatbots - Sentiment Analysis -
Translation Systems - Question Answering

3.4 Computer Vision Enables machines to interpret and understand visual
data. Applications: - Object Detection - Facial Recognition - Medical
Imaging

3.5 Robotics Combines AI with mechanical systems for automation and
intelligent control.

-   Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
